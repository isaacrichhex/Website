The Problem

Graphitization Is
Holding Batteries Back

Synthetic graphite powers the anodes of virtually every lithium-ion EV battery. Making it requires graphitization — an energy-intensive heat treatment step that the industry has performed the same way for decades.

Energy Intensive

The graphitization step consumes enormous amounts of electricity per tonne of product — making it the single largest cost driver in the anode supply chain.

Large Footprint

Conventional batch furnaces are massive, capital-intensive installations that are slow to commission and difficult to scale in pace with EV demand growth.

High Emissions

Each tonne of synthetic graphite produced by conventional methods generates substantial CO₂ equivalent emissions — a burden on the clean energy transition it is meant to enable.

Demand Is Exploding

Global demand for battery-grade synthetic graphite is projected to grow rapidly through 2038. New, more efficient production technology is urgently needed.

Our Solution

A Better Way to
Make Graphite

Hexoris is developing a compact, continuous high-temperature reactor platform. By rethinking the heating mechanism and process flow, we are targeting substantial reductions in energy use relative to existing technology — while also reducing reactor footprint and cycle time.

Continuous Flow Design

Eliminates batch cycle overhead, reducing manual handling and improving throughput and safety.

Electric Arc Heating

Reaches extreme temperatures with dramatically less waste heat than conventional resistance furnaces.

Compact Reactor Volume

A smaller physical footprint per tonne of output lowers capex requirements for new facilities.

Biomass Feed Pathway

Being developed with a long-term pathway to process biomass-derived carbon precursors.
