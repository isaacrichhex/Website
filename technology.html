Technology

Continuous High-Temperature
Reactors, Reinvented

Hexoris is developing a compact, continuous high-temperature reactor platform targeting substantially lower energy consumption and reduced capital requirements compared to conventional batch processing — starting with battery-grade synthetic graphite.

Target Markets

One Platform, Multiple Markets

The same fundamental problem — energy-intensive, capital-intensive batch high-temperature processing — exists across a wide range of advanced materials. The Hexoris platform is being designed with this breadth in mind.

Synthetic Graphite

Battery-grade synthetic graphite for EV battery anodes is our first commercial target. Conventional graphitization is among the most energy- and capital-intensive steps in the battery materials supply chain.

Primary Focus

Silicon Carbide (SiC)

SiC is a critical material for power electronics, EV inverters, and semiconductor applications. Current production relies on the Acheson batch furnace process — a century-old approach with significant energy and capital intensity.

Target Market

Boron Carbide (B₄C)

One of the hardest and lightest materials known, B₄C is a critical defence material — used in ceramic body armour plates (SAPI/ESAPI), vehicle and aircraft armour, and nuclear reactor control systems. It is produced in energy-intensive electric arc batch furnaces, with no commercially viable continuous alternative currently available.

Target Market

Zirconium Carbide (ZrC)

ZrC is a high-value refractory material with critical defence and strategic applications — including nuclear fuel cladding for advanced reactors, hypersonic vehicle thermal protection, and rocket propulsion components. It is synthesized via batch high-temperature reduction processes with substantial energy and capital costs.

Target Market

Natural Graphite Purification

High-purity natural graphite for battery and industrial use typically requires batch thermal or chemical purification at high temperatures — a process that shares the same fundamental inefficiencies the Hexoris platform is designed to address.

Target Market

Biomass-Derived Graphite

A long-term pathway to process biomass-derived carbon precursors is being developed, enabling a lower-carbon feedstock option across the synthetic graphite and specialty carbon value chain.

Comparison

Hexoris vs. Conventional Batch Processing

Hexoris Platform
  • Continuous flow — no batch reload cycles
  • Electric arc heating — extreme graphitization temperatures
  • Compact thermal zone — low waste heat losses
  • Targeting substantial energy reduction vs. conventional
  • Simpler reactor design — fewer components, lower build cost
  • Reduced capital requirements — smaller, more accessible installations
  • Smaller reactor footprint per tonne of output
  • Reduced safety risk — no large batch handling
  • Biomass precursor pathway in development
VS
Conventional Technology
  • Large batch furnaces with long cycle times
  • Resistance heating — lower operating temperatures
  • Significant heat loss over long heating cycles
  • Very high electricity consumption per tonne
  • Large physical installations with high capital costs
  • Complex infrastructure raises barrier to entry and scale-up cost
  • Robotic or manual batch loading required
  • Petroleum-derived precursors only
